2. When Do Plants Produce Ethylene?

Ethylene is mainly produced when:

  • A plant is under stress ---> Abscission
  • A plant organ is aging ---> Senescence
  • A fruit is ripening

3. Leaf Abscission

Abscission means:

  • Shedding
  • Loss of plant organs
  • Most commonly, loss of leaves

A very familiar example is autumn season.

When environmental conditions become stressful:

  • Ethylene concentration increases
  • This increased ethylene promotes leaf abscission

4. Senescence

Senescence means:

  • Aging of plant tissues
  • Gradual degeneration
  • Loss of function
  • Eventual death of the organ

When plant tissues become old:

  • Ethylene is produced
  • Ethylene promotes further senescence

5. Growth in Seedlings (Triple Response)

Now let us understand how ethylene affects young plants.

When a seed germinates underground:

  • The seedling is surrounded by soil
  • Ethylene gas cannot escape easily
  • Ethylene concentration increases around the seedling

This causes a special set of responses called the triple response.

Ethylene causes:

  1. Horizontal growth of seedlings

6. Root Growth

Ethylene:

  • Promotes root growth
  • Promotes root hair formation

7. Ethylene and Dormancy (Seeds and Buds)

Dormancy means:

  • A resting phase
  • No growth even if the seed or bud is alive

Ethylene plays an important role in ending dormancy.

Ethylene:

  • Breaks seed dormancy
  • Breaks bud dormancy

examples:

  • Initiates germination in peanut seeds
  • Promotes sprouting of potato tubers

8. Ethylene and Shoot Growth in Deep-Water Rice

This is a very important adaptive role.

In deep-water rice:

  • Plants grow in flooded conditions
  • Water covers most of the shoot

In such conditions:

  • Ethylene gas gets trapped
  • Ethylene concentration increases

This causes:

  • Rapid internode elongation
  • Rapid petiole elongation

As a result:

  • Leaves
  • Upper parts of the shoot

remain above water.

9. Fruit Ripening

Fruit ripening is one of the most important roles of ethylene.

Ethylene is:

  • Highly effective in fruit ripening

During ripening:

  • Ethylene level increases
  • Respiration rate increases sharply

This sudden increase in respiration is called: Respiratory climacteric

Ethylene causes:

  • Softening of fruits
  • Conversion of starch into sugars
  • Development of colour and flavour

10. Agricultural Uses of Ethylene

Because ethylene controls many processes, it is widely used.

Ethylene is used:

  • To initiate flowering
  • To synchronise fruit set in pineapples
  • To induce flowering in mango

11. Ethephon

Using ethylene gas directly is difficult.

So farmers use ethephon.

Ethephon:

  • Is used as a source of ethylene
  • Is readily absorbed in aqueous solution
  • Is transported inside the plant
  • Releases ethylene slowly

Uses of ethephon:

  • Hastens fruit ripening in tomatoes and apples

  • Accelerates abscission in flowers and fruits

    • Used for thinning of cotton, cherry, walnut

  • Promotes female flowers in cucumber

    • This increases yield
